# Red Team Research Blog

This site presents independent research on adversary tradecraft, detection evasion, and defensive blind spots, developed through controlled lab experimentation and long-form technical analysis.

The focus is on **how modern attack chains are constructed**, **why certain detections fail**, and **what defenders can realistically observe and mitigate**â€”bridging the gap between red teaming, reverse engineering, and detection engineering.

Content may include:
- High-level analysis of multi-stage attack chains  
- Defensive implications of publicly disclosed vulnerabilities  
- Research diagrams and annotated threat models  
- Video demonstrations hosted on external platforms  
- Commentary on detection limits and analyst workload  

All material is produced for **educational and defensive research purposes** and avoids operational deployment guidance.

## About the Author

Chang Tan is an independent cybersecurity researcher, author, and trainer with a focus on red teaming, detection evasion analysis, and hybrid warfare concepts.

He is the author of *Ultimate Cyberwarfare for Evasive Cyber Tactics* and related works examining the intersection of adversary tradecraft, defensive limitations, and modern geopolitical dynamics. His work emphasizes technical realism, historical grounding, and analytical clarity over tooling-centric instruction.

In addition to research and writing, Chang has developed and delivered training on red team methodologies, adversary behavior analysis, and the strategic context surrounding cyber and hybrid conflict.
